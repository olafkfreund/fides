Why it makes ServiceNow better

The gaps ServiceNow leaves open — closed with tamper-evidence

ServiceNow's DevOps Change Velocity auto-approves on pipeline metrics alone: no cryptographic evidence, no build-vs-deploy distinction, GRC evidence uploaded by hand. Every one of those is a Fides strength.

Evidence-driven CAB & Now Assist

Signed evidence bundle + 0–100 risk + control-coverage land on the exact record the CAB reviews — votes stop being guesses.

Governed, not scraping

Reads flow through ServiceNow's MCP layer, so its ACLs still apply. Fides can't see what the identity isn't entitled to.

Kills the GRC silo

change_request and sn_compliance_control reference the same Fides attestation — change → control → audit in one hop.

Two things rivals can't copy fast

An AI-native (MCP) control plane and a self-hostable, tamper-evident chain — neither Kosli (SaaS-only) nor JFrog AppTrust has both.

How it's wired

Two commands and a scripted call

The MCP client and grounding endpoint ship in Fides. ServiceNow needs one service account (governed read + change write-back) and one Scripted REST resource to feed Now Assist.

Fides side · connect once

# point Fides at the instance (secret is a reference, never inline)
fides servicenow config --instance-url https://acme.service-now.com \
    --auth-type oauth2 --client-id <id> --secret-path SN_OAUTH_SECRET

# prove it, both directions
fides servicenow mcp lookup --table change_request --limit 3
fides servicenow grounding --change CHG0032508
